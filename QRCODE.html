Scanning and printing of QR codes

Can I add a QR code to my website (and will people be able to scan it)? expand_more

You can add QR codes to pretty much anything you can think of, from physical goods and locations to digital properties like websites, applications, emails and social media profiles. People will be able to scan it with any compatible device.

How do you scan a QR code? expand_more

The majority of Android and iOS smartphones are equipped with an in-built QR code reader within their camera. However, if your smartphone lacks this feature, you can download an app that enables your device to read QR codes.

How do I print my QR code? expand_more

Because your QR code will be exported in high resolution, you'll be able to use it however you want to. After downloading it in the desired format, you can print it from your computer, and you can also supply that file to your graphic designers so they can add it to your packaging and your other printed materials.

What data is collected in dynamic QR code scans? expand_more

Our dynamic QR code scans collect a range of key information about the performance of your QR codes, such as how often they're being scanned and where and how they're being scanned. You'll be able to see information like the devices and the operating systems that people are using to scan your QR codes. You'll also be able to see how many times your codes have been scanned, as well as how many unique people have scanned them. Get more insights on your customers with our easy-to-use statistics and charts or easily export your data to Excel.

Do QR codes have a scan counter? expand_more

Yes. Our comprehensive analytics tracks the number of scans that a QR code receives, as well as when, where and what devices scanned the QR code.
